# The Standard Board Meeting Agenda

A board meeting isn't for reporting basic metrics; it's for leveraging the collective brainpower of the room. Always send your "board deck" (metrics, financials, product updates) 48 to 72 hours in advance so everyone arrives ready to debate, not just listen.

**Standard 90 to 120-minute early-stage agenda:**

| Time | Agenda Item | Purpose |
|------|-------------|---------|
| 5–10 min | Administrative | Approve previous minutes and vote on routine items (e.g., standard equity grants, minor contracts). |
| 15–20 min | CEO & Business Update | Exception-based reporting on burn rate, runway, and core adoption metrics. Highlight what went right, what went wrong, and what surprised you. |
| 45–60 min | Strategic Discussion | The core of the meeting. Focus on 1–2 major challenges where you need advice (e.g., GTM positioning, security features, pricing models). |
| 15–20 min | Functional Deep Dive | A rotating spotlight on a specific area (e.g., product roadmap, engineering architecture), often led by a key team member. |
| 10 min | Closed Session | Investors and independent directors meet without founders to discuss governance, CEO performance, and alignment. |

> **Key insight:** Never present your deck slide-by-slide. Use the meeting time for discussion, debate, and problem-solving. Every strategic agenda item should have a specific question attached to it.
