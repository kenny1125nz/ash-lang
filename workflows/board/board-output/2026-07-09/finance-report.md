## 1. Executive Summary

Ash exits Q2 2026 with **$4.4M cash on hand** and an average monthly burn of **$310K**, yielding a **14-month runway** — a contraction from 19 months last quarter driven by headcount expansion and infrastructure scaling for Ash Cloud. Revenue remains pre-revenue in any material sense ($400 MRR), and the company is entirely dependent on cash reserves until enterprise contracts convert.

The quarter's financial narrative is best understood through the lens of our three mission pillars:

| Pillar | Q2 Progress | Financial Implication |
|--------|------------|----------------------|
| **Own the Standard** | GitHub stars doubled (4.1K → 8.2K); monthly active workflows grew 271% (1.4K → 5.2K); unique organizations grew 150% (600 → 1,500). The `.md`-native format is gaining network effects. | Adoption velocity supports the thesis but has not yet monetized. Every new org is a future pipeline entry. |
| **Commoditize the Supplier** | `ash discover` shipped (zero-config onboarding); Markdown workflow engine prototype complete; context compaction in progress. Architecture remains model-agnostic. | Infrastructure spend ($28K/mo) will rise as execution volume scales. These are strategic, non-discretionary costs. |
| **Eliminate Enterprise Liability** | 6 enterprise evaluations in progress; EU AI Act enforcement (Jan 2027) directly supports our audit-trail value proposition; SOC 2 Type II flagged as blocking criterion across all evaluations. | Zero revenue today; pipeline represents $600K total ACV (risk-adjusted ~$82K). Every deal is gated on security posture. |

**Headline judgment:** The company is executing well against its mission but is spending faster than it is converting. Runway pressure will become acute in H2 2026 if enterprise deals do not close on schedule. The board should view the next two quarters as the critical monetization window.

### 5.2 Pipeline Diagnosis

- **No deal has passed security review.** This is the single largest blocker. All 6 accounts are pre-close; none have cleared the enterprise gate. SOC 2 Type II was cited as a requirement in every evaluation.
- **FintechCo is the bellwether.** At security review with a 30% probability, it is the nearest-term, highest-confidence deal. If FintechCo stalls or rejects, the signal will cascade to the other 5 evaluations.
- **Weighted pipeline ($81,500) is immaterial against burn ($310K/mo).** Even if the entire weighted pipeline converted tomorrow, it would fund less than 2 additional weeks of operations.

**Mission lens — Eliminate Enterprise Liability:** The pipeline is stuck exactly where the mission predicts. Enterprises want Ash's governance and audit trail, but they cannot buy it until we prove we are not a liability ourselves. The EU AI Act's January 2027 enforcement deadline is a tailwind — it creates a regulatory forcing function for exactly the product we are building. The question is whether we can clear SOC 2 and security review before the market window opens. Recommendation: accelerate SOC 2 Type II engagement immediately.

## 7. Risks & Recommendations

### 7.1 Financial Risks

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Enterprise deals slip past Q4 2026 | **High** | No deal has cleared security review. Begin fundraising prep now; do not wait for a close. |
| Burn increase from hiring plan | **Medium** | Defer Account Executive hire until first enterprise close; prioritize Developer Advocate (community leverage) over AE. |
| Infrastructure cost scaling | **Low** | $28K/mo is manageable; Ash Cloud's 99.7% uptime is strong. Context compaction will reduce per-workflow compute costs. |
| Key-person risk (Marcus, parser/engine) | **High** | Marcus is irreplaceable on the core execution engine. Mitigation: hire or train a backup engineer for the parser before Q4. |
| SOC 2 / security posture | **High** | Blocking all enterprise revenue. Single-region deployment, no DR plan, no dedicated security engineer. The CTO handling security part-time is unsustainable given the pipeline volume. |

### 7.2 Strategic Recommendations

1. **Accelerate SOC 2 Type II engagement (Q3).** Every enterprise evaluation is blocked on it. The cost (~$40-80K and 4-6 months) is a necessary investment that directly unlocks the $600K pipeline and every future enterprise deal. This is the highest-ROI spend the company can make outside of engineering.
2. **Begin Series A prep by October 2026.** Even in the optimistic case (2 enterprise closes), runway reaches critical levels by mid-2027. Fundraising from a position of demonstrated enterprise traction is far stronger than from a position of need.
3. **Defer Account Executive hire until first enterprise close.** A $355K/mo burn rate (post-hires) is acceptable; an AE without a product that has cleared enterprise security review is a cost without a target.
4. **Allocate engineering time to the #1 complaint: long workflow failures.** The context-compaction milestone (targeting August) is the bridge between the self-serve product and enterprise-scale workloads. It is directly on the critical path for **Eliminate Enterprise Liability**.
5. **Exploit the EU AI Act window.** January 2027 enforcement is a hard deadline for European enterprises that matches our audit-trail value proposition. Consider a targeted EU enterprise push in Q4 2026 with compliance-specific messaging.
