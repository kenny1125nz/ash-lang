# Board Meeting Deliverables

Four key deliverables are produced from each board meeting:

## 1. The Meeting Minutes

The minutes are the official, permanent, and legally binding record of the meeting. They do not function as a verbatim transcript of the debate, but rather as a focused summary of the proceedings.

- **What they include:** Attendance, declarations of conflicts of interest, a high-level summary of the discussions, and the exact wording of all decisions made.
- **Why they matter:** They prove that the directors exercised due diligence, acted in good faith, and complied with corporate governance laws.

## 2. Formal Resolutions

Resolutions are the exact, documented decisions that the board has formally agreed upon, often separated from the narrative of the minutes for ease of reference.

- **What they include:** Clear, unambiguous statements of action, typically beginning with "RESOLVED THAT...".
- **Why they matter:** They provide the legal authority for the company to execute major actions, such as appointing executives, signing significant contracts, issuing stock, or approving budgets.

## 3. Action Items and Mandates

Decisions made in the boardroom must be translated into operational reality for the company's management.

- **What they include:** An action item list (often tracked under "Matters Arising") that assigns specific tasks to the CEO, management team, or individual directors, complete with clear deadlines.
- **Why they matter:** They ensure accountability and form the basis for the management team's immediate priorities. The status of these items is typically the first thing reviewed at the next board meeting.

## 4. Approved Policies and Reports

The board is responsible for reviewing and ratifying key organizational documents.

- **What they include:** Signed-off annual financial statements, updated corporate policies (like risk management or compliance frameworks), strategic plans, and committee reports.
- **Why they matter:** Once approved by the board, these documents become the official, governing guidelines for the organization.
