# Board Directors

When filling independent board seats, look for people who cover your blind spots and have successfully navigated the specific challenges of developer tools and AI workflows:


## Executive Directors (Internal)

Who they are: The CEO, the Founders, and sometimes other top executives (like the CFO or COO).

Their role: They represent the day-to-day management of the company. They bring the on-the-ground operational reality to the board's strategic discussions.

Investor Directors (External)

Who they are: Representatives from the venture capital (VC) firms or angel groups that have put significant money into the company. Usually, the lead investor of a major funding round gets a dedicated seat.

Their role: They represent the financial interests of the shareholders. Their primary focus is ensuring the company scales efficiently, manages its cash, and eventually reaches a profitable exit (like an acquisition or IPO).

## Independent Directors (The "Experts")

Who they are: The outside experts you mentioned. These are industry veterans, former CEOs, or specialized consultants who do not work for the company and aren't tied to a specific investor.

Their role: To provide unbiased, strategic advice and act as a neutral tie-breaker between founders and investors.

### The DevTools Go-To-Market (GTM) Expert

Building an orchestration language is very different from selling one. You want someone who has successfully driven bottom-up developer adoption and transitioned that momentum into top-down enterprise sales.

### The Open-Source / Community Veteran

If you plan to make parts of the project open-source, an executive who has built and monetized developer communities (think HashiCorp, Vercel, or Docker) will be invaluable for navigating licensing, community relations, and paid tier structures.

### The Enterprise Architecture Leader

Enterprises care deeply about reliability, auditability, and deterministic execution when it comes to AI agents. A former CTO or VP of Engineering who understands enterprise security and compliance will lend massive credibility when pitching the workflows to large organizations.
