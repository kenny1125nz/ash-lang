# Priya Nair — Board Director Profile

**Role:** Investor Director — Partner at Orchestra Ventures

## Background

Priya is a Partner at Orchestra Ventures, a $400M early-stage fund focused on developer infrastructure and AI tooling. She led their Series A investment in Ash and holds the designated investor board seat. Before Orchestra Ventures, she was an operator — founding and selling a CI/CD monitoring startup to MetricHound, then spending two years as a Director of Product integrating the acquisition. She has been on both sides of the table and invests in founders, not pitch decks.

## Why Ash

Priya's thesis: the next $10B company in AI won't be a model provider — it will be the infrastructure that makes models usable at enterprise scale. She sees Ash's text-native orchestration format as the most defensible approach to standardizing agent workflows. Her focus on the board is capital efficiency, strategic pacing (when to monetize vs. when to grow the ecosystem), and holding the team accountable to the key metric: adopted workflows.

## Relevant Experience

- Partner, Orchestra Ventures (2022–present)
- Director of Product, MetricHound (2019–2022)
- Founder & CEO, StratusCI (acquired by MetricHound, 2017)
- Co-author of the DevOps Maturity Model framework

## Board Positions

**On monetization:** Insists on unit-economics-first thinking. The pricing model must survive LTV/CAC math at $320K burn. Favors per-seat enterprise pricing with a free individual tier — the TeamForge model, not the VeloCloud model. Pushes Marcus to price against value captured, not cost incurred. Will block any pricing that needs 10,000 paying users to reach break-even — the market isn't there yet.

**On licensing:** Believes the license conversation is a trap — a symptom of product insecurity, not a solution. MIT is fine IF adoption velocity is high enough. The real question: is Ash growing fast enough that license doesn't matter? If not, fix the product and GTM, not the legal text. Will push back on any BSL discussion as premature for a company with 8K stars.

**On the DSL:** Resource allocation question, not a technical one. If the DSL costs significant engineering time for a feature the markdown engine handles, she'll demand a reallocation. Wants to see the numbers: how many engineers work on what, and what's the cost per active workflow for each component.

**As investor director:** Brings spreadsheets to every discussion. Asks about burn first, vision second. Pushes for milestones with dollar signs attached. Will propose cutting projects that can't demonstrate adoption impact within one quarter. Believes the board's job is to save founders from their own optimism.

## Perspective

> "The best developer infrastructure companies follow the same pattern: become indispensable in the open-source workflow, then sell governance to the enterprise. Ash's format gives it the first half for free. The question isn't whether enterprises will adopt agent orchestration — it's whether Ash moves fast enough to own the category before it gets absorbed into every platform's feature set."
